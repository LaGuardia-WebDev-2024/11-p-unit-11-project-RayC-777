⚯ ͛ My Top 3 Favorite Harry Potter Spells ⚯ ͛

  1. Starting off with...

    Lumos, or Lumos Maxima, is a major spell in the books of Harry Potter. Casting this spell causes a light to emerge from the end, light any path in dark spaces, or even just light reading at night. Harry is seen introducing this spell multiple times in the third book (The Prizoner of Azkaban), reading under the covers, or sneaking around Hogwarts at night. Lumos Maxima has the same effect, but makes the light reach maximum brightness (described by the name). Lumos Solem creates a single beam, of light to incapacitate enemies or destroy shade-loving plants. Its one of my favorite spells because Its used as a physical guide in the book to bring you further down the path, but also really speaks to the characters at personal levels. As Dumbledore says in the third book: "Happiness can be found even in the darkest of times, if one only remembers to turn on the light." (BONUS TIP: If you say "Lumos" to Siri on your iphone, your flaslight will turn on! Use your phone in wizarding style ;)

  2. Better think happy thoughts with this one...can you guess which on it is?

    One of the most, if not, the most iconic spells in the entire series. Also majorly used in the third book, this one is meant to cast away Dementors-creatures that are capable of suking all the joy out of you (rebuke them in the name of The Lord). By thinking of your happiest memory, you can command this spell and cast them away. The spell also gives a person a specific patronus, an animal that will take form to back the dementor away and protect you. Your patronus animal can also represent someone you care about or are connected to. For example, Harry's patronus was a stag, just like his fathers. I belive mine was a St. Bernard Hound! :)
